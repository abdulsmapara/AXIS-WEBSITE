Techno.docx


About

Call to attention the amazing technological developments taking place in our country, and inspire young minds to think, learn and create.
Techno.docx is a unique platform for all the budding researchers out there to showcase their innovative and original research or project ideas to a wider audience, and get their proposals appraised by some of the brightest and most astute minds in India. Do you have it in you to pass our rigorous and critical review to open up the portal to true greatness?

Problem Statement

Participate in this event by submitting your technical papers that may fall under any of the following Engineering Disciplines:
  • Computer Science and Information Technology
  • Chemical,Metallurgy and Material Science
  • Mechanical and Mechatronics
  • Electronics and Electrical
  • Civil and Architecture

    • Game Play

  • This round is 'Online Abstract Submission'.
  • Participants must register at www.axisvnit.org.
  • POINT TO BE ADDED SUBMISSION OF DATE
  • The abstract must be submitted mentioning name of the event as the subject of mail, names of team members, contact numbers, email id at technodocx@axisvnit.org

    • Round 2:
  • This is the 'On-Stage paper presentation'
  • Shortlisted students are expected to present their work on-stage in front of a panel of judges at VNIT Nagpur.
  • Maximum time limit for the presentation is 8 minutes and for the Q&A sessionis 3 minutes.

    • Rules and Regulations

  • A maximum of 2 students per team are allowed to present a paper (one as author and another as co-author).
  • In case of team participation, both the members are required to be present on the spot at the time of the event.
  • Subject of the mail for draft should contain name of author, co-author (at least one co-author)and their AXIS ID along with project or research paper name.
  • Subject of mail should be: "Project or Research Paper Title - Author Name (AXIS ID), Co-author Name (AXIS ID)"
    • "
  • Only genuine and original paper abstracts should be uploaded. Abstracts found copied from any source like internet/ journals etc. will be immediately disqualified.
  • The presentations should be in .ppt or .pdf format. The presentation room is equipped with LCD projector.
  • The time limit for one presentation will be 8 minutes and Q&A session for at most 3 minutes. A buzzer will be rung after 6 minutes to remind the participants about the time and a final buzzer will be rung at the end of 8 minutes. Any extension in the allotted time will result in loss of points.
  • No requests regarding change in schedule of the paper presentation will be entertained under any circumstances.
  • The abstract as well as the papers will be judged on the basis of their innovation, knowledge of the field and presentation skills. The decision of the judges will be final and binding.
  • Abstract and soft copy of the research paper should be submitted before the last date of 1st round of Techno.Docx which is to be decided yet. No late submissions will be accepted.
  • Shortlisted students will be informed within 1 week of the last date of submission of abstract.
  • Students should carry soft and 2 hard copies of their report during the presentation. Ppt and hardcopy should be submitted to the organisers before the commencement of the event.
  • Result will be displayed shortly after the end of the event.

    • Guidelines

    1. Abstract
  • Objectives
  • Beneficiaries (For whom)
  • Value of results (Use)

    2. Research
  • Present methods of tackling the problem (if any)
  • Proposed Solution
  • Alternate solutions /approaches
  • Novelty of Approach: How is your solution better than existing products that address the same problem?

    3. Technical Report
  • Description of concepts, theories or approach involved in the proposed solution.
  • Detailed technical specifications and pictorial representations (block diagrams)
  • Description of the flow of operations demonstrating key features and functionality
  • Technical aspect of the proposed solution.
  • Performance estimate of the solution.
  • Experimentation done to establish the workability of the above
  • A link to the video of the working model/ prototype.

    4. Result
  • Actual findings, significant output of tests and analysis (Must be readable)
  • Include problems encountered, believability of results, accuracy estimates
  • Pros and Cons of your solution
  • Utility of results

    5. Application
  • Your idea as a solution to the problem
  • Additional applications
  • Benefits to the users
