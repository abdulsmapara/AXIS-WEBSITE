SUNITA WILLIAMS
AXIS'19 is extremely honoured to host Hon'ble Sunita L. Williams as the guest for the live webinar session.

A woman of flawless mettle, Ms Sunita Williams is an Indian-American Astronaut and the United States Navy Officer of Indo-Slovenian descent. She was selected as an astronaut by NASA in 1998 and is a veteran of two space missions Expeditions 14/15 and 32/33. She is currently training for the first post-certification mission of Boeing’s Starliner spacecraft – the second crewed flight for that vehicle – and her third long duration mission aboard the International Space Station.

Williams and her crewmates are working closely with Boeing to develop their new spacecraft systems, which will provide roundtrip crew transportation services to the International Space Station and, along with SpaceX’s CrewDragon, return the ability to launch humans into space from United States soil.

She is a former record holder for total spacewalks by a woman, total spacewalk time by a woman and the first person to run a marathon in space.

AXIS'19 is honoured to host its guest for the QnA- Knowing the Unknown, Col Tejendra Pal Tyagi and Neelam Saxena Chandra.

QnA-Knowing the Unknown is aimed at bridging the gap between leaders of today and tomorrow by hosting passionate, successful and eminent personalities through one-of-a-kind format which would help the youth understand why the 'path less travelled by' is an important one

Neelam Saxena Chandra :
She is an Indian poet and author. She has received several awards including the Rabindranath Tagore International Award in 2014 given by Xpress Publications: Kerala, India. An IES Officer of 1993 batch, she has also served as Joint Secretary of Union Public Service Commission (UPSC).She is currently serving as General Manager of Pune Metro.

Col Tejendra Pal Tyagi :

JAAP HAARTSEN
Jaap C. Haartsen (Feb1963) has been active in the area of Wireless Communications for more than 25 years.In 1994, he laid the foundations for the system that later became known as Bluetooth Wireless Technology. He is internationally regarded as the Father of Bluetooth. In May 2015, he was inducted in the National Inventor Hall of Fame (NIHF) at the USPTO, Washington for his work on the Bluetooth technology.
